What is a Car?

A car, or an automobile, is a motor vehicle with wheels. Most definitions of cars state that they run primarily on roads, seat one to eight people, have four wheels, and mainly transport people rather than cargo.

Car Body Style

A car's body style is the shape and design of the vehicle

  • Sedan has four doors and a traditional trunk.
  • Coupe has historically been considered a two-door car with a trunk and a solid roof.
  • Sports are two-seaters, but sometimes have small rear seats as well.
  • Wagons are similar to sedans but have an extended roofline and a hatch door at the rear instead of a trunk.
  • Hatchback a compact or subcompact sedan with a squared-off roof and a rear flip-up hatch door that provides access to the vehicle's cargo area instead of a conventional trunk.
  • Convertibles are two seaters or more with a retractable/removeable roof.
  • SUVs—often also referred to as crossovers—tend to be taller and boxier than sedans, offer an elevated seating position, and have more ground clearance than a car.
  • Minivans are the workhorses of the family-car world, the best at carrying people and cargo in an efficient package.
  • Pickup truck has a passenger cab and an open cargo bed in the rear.
